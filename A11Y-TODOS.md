## TODOs

### 1. Fix `.link` contrast in navbar (affects **all routes**)

**Rule:** `color-contrast` · **Impact:** serious  
**Why:** A `.link` element in the app shell (likely `auth-menu.component.html` or nav links) has foreground/background contrast below WCAG 2 AA (4.5:1).  
**Fix:** Replace `class="link"` with an explicit foreground color that passes contrast, e.g. add `text-base-content` or `text-primary-content` depending on the background it sits on. Check the exact element in DevTools.

### 2. Fix card text contrast on `/showcase`

**Rule:** `color-contrast` · **Impact:** serious  
**Affected elements:**
- `h1` (page heading) — on the `#f0f0f0` body background
- `.text-lg` (subtitle paragraph) — on the `#f0f0f0` body background
- `.tab-active`, `.tab:nth-child(2)`, `.tab:nth-child(3)` — tab bar on `#f0f0f0`
- `h2` (item card title) — inside `.item-card` on `bg-base-100`
- `.text-base-content/70` (item description) — opacity-reduced text fails on white
- `.badge-outline.badge-success` (Free badge) — low contrast outlined green badge
- `.text-base-content/50` ("Sign in to call dibs") — opacity-reduced text fails
- `.text-base-content/60` (claimed-by text) — opacity-reduced text fails

**Likely root cause:** The body background was changed to `#f0f0f0` (from `bg-base-100: #fafafa`). The DaisyUI opacity utilities (`/50`, `/60`, `/70`) were calibrated for the lighter base. Two things need fixing:

- **Header area:** The `h1` and subtitle render directly on `#f0f0f0`. Either revert the body bg or darken the text tokens.
- **Card text:** Opacity-modulated classes like `text-base-content/70` fail even on white cards. Replace the lowest-opacity variants with explicit accessible colors, or increase opacity thresholds (e.g. `/70` → `/80`, `/50` → `/65`).
- **Free badge:** `badge-success badge-outline` — the green border on white is too low contrast. Consider using a filled `badge-success` instead of outline.
